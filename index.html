We are reinventing the retail industry through innovative technology.

We're a full-stack startup creating unique solutions in data-driven merchandising, massively scaled personal styling, and complex logistics. We value very lightweight planning, continuous deployment, little tech debt, no product managers.

Engineering

Our multi-app, event-driven application architecture drives both scale and flexibility.

Many applications and services

There are no monoliths here. Each application in our platform has a clear and distinct purpose and all of them are built in house. That means small, clean code bases with little technical debt and lots of flexibility. We use RabbitMQ to send messages about real-world events to other apps which decide whether or not to do something with them. This de-coupled, asynchronous architecture allows us to scale with ease and add or change components as needed. When it really makes sense we will build a RESTful API to wrap a service requiring an immediate response.

Product is our primary concern

We hire engineers with great Product instincts because we are also the Product team: building great software is a collaborative effort so we work directly with stakeholders in the business. We are a central part of the business so we deliver real value and not just features, and because we understand how Stitch Fix works we are trusted to focus our time and effort where they are most needed.

Deadlines are doomed to fail

Starting a conversation with a due date dooms it to fail from the beginning. Deadlines don't get things done faster but they do encourage stress, bad decisions, and technical debt. We still deliver a lot in a short period of time, but we don't burn ourselves out in the process.

Some of what we work on

Personal Styling
  • Helping our stylists leverage our very effective styling algorithm to put together incredible, unique fixes for all our clients
  • Our stylists need to see the best possible matches between each client and all our clothes, and they need to use that information with their own knowledge to create a unique fix for each of our customers
  • We need to give our stylists and their managers insight into their performance and success rates
Operations and Warehouse
  • We receive lots of deliveries from vendors every day and need to get them into our physical and digital inventory as quickly as possible
  • We need to pick, pack and ship lots of Fixes every day, and they all need to be of the highest quality
  • We get lots of returns from clients every day (it’s part of the business model) and we have to check the merchandise and get it back into inventory as quickly as possible
Merchandising
  • We have to order lots of new inventory every day and synchronize those orders with the warehouse
  • We have to plan every upcoming week, month, season and year to ensure we have the right amount and best mix of clothes
  • We have to leverage all of our sales and client data to make the best possible buying decisions
Customer Service
  • We encourage our customers to give us feedback about all aspects of our service through whichever channels they want, and we need to keep track of all of that
  • We want our service to be as convenient as possible and sometimes that means lending a helping hand with shipments, payments or using our website

More about Stitch Fix Engineering

We use technologies like PostgreSQL, Redis, ElasticSearch and RabbitMQ along with various private Gems to tie them all together. We work from San Francisco, Pittsburgh and remotely, and we use Heroku, GitHub, Google Hangouts and HipChat to help us build it all.

We are looking for people who share our values. We like to move quickly and ship lots of code, but in a considered way. We use TDD, CI and an all-hands-on-deck approach to Pull Requests to ensure we can keep the pace up without stinging on quality.

If any of us have a good idea for introducing a new technology or pattern then we go ahead and run an experiment. The debates don’t start until we have some real evidence to look at and since we avoid dogma and evangelism they tend to be short.

We work closely with our internal partners and we assume that they know best about what they need to do their jobs. Our job is to solve business problems and some of our proudest moments come from solving those problems without a line of code.

Culture

Bright, Kind, Goal-oriented.

Bright

We have really interesting problems to solve due to our unique business model and hire really bright people to tackle them. If it were easy everyone would be doing it, right? This means more than just book smarts. We don't start with proposed solutions, we start with problems and goals and work collaboratively to come to the right solution.

Kind

It's not just being clever that makes you a good fit at Stitch Fix. We really value how well our team works together and kindness a huge part of it. Working with people we enjoy being around and that make us excited to start work each day is a large part of our success.

Goal-oriented

We love people who are driven and self-motivated. We are a largely flat organization and value autonomy combined with results. No one likes to be micro-managed and we don't want to manage people that way either.

TL;DR

In short, we get stuff done. We have fun. We test. We learn. We work well together. We think big. We care about the product we are building. We push boundaries.

We love what we do and we take the time to do it right.
