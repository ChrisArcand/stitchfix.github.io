Engineering

Our multi-app, event-driven application architecture drives both scale and flexibility.

Multiple application architecture

There are no monoliths here. Each application in our platform has a clear and distinct purpose and all of them are built in house, primarily in Ruby. That means small, clean code bases with little technical debt and lots of flexibility. Often we use RabbitMQ to send messages about real-world events to other apps who decide for themselves whether or not to do something with it. This de-coupled architecture allows us to scale with ease and add or change components as needed. When it really makes sense we build RESTful API's for our services as well.

We are the Product team

We hire engineers with great Product instinct because we are also the Product team. We value practicality and efficiency over process and interface directly with stakeholders in the business. Building great software is a collaborative effort. The business knows we need to do more than just deliver features and trusts where we focus our time and effort.

Deadlines are doomed to fail

Starting a conversation with a due date dooms it to fail from the beginning. We don't believe deadlines get things done faster. Rather, they encourage stress, bad decisions, and technical debt. We still deliver a lot in a short period of time, but not because we burned ourselves out in the process.

Some of what we work on

Operations and Warehouse
  • We receive lots of deliveries from vendors every day and need to get them into our physical and digital inventory as quickly as possible
  • We need to pick, pack and ship lots of Fixes every day, and they all need to be of the highest quality
  • We get lots of returns from clients every day (it’s part of the business model) and we have to check the merchandise and get it back into inventory as quickly as possible
Merchandising
  • We have to order lots of new inventory every day and synchronize those orders with the warehouse
  • We have to plan every upcoming week, month, season and year to ensure we have the right amount and best mix of clothes
  • We have to leverage all of our sales and client data to make the best possible buying decisions
Personal Styling
  • Helping our stylists leverage our very effective styling algorithm to put together incredible, unique fixes for all our clients
  • Our stylists need to see the best possible matches between each client and all our clothes, and they need to use that information with their own knowledge to create a unique fix for each of our customers
  • We need to give our stylists and their managers insight into their performance and success rates
Customer Service
  • We encourage our customers to give us feedback about all aspects of our service through whichever channels they want, and we need to keep track of all of that
  • We want our service to be as convenient as possible and sometimes that means lending a helping hand with shipments, payments or using our website

More about Stitch Fix Engineering

We use technologies like PostgreSQL, Redis, ElasticSearch and RabbitMQ along with various private Gems to tie them all together. We work from San Francisco, Pittsburgh and remotely, and we use Heroku, GitHub, Google Hangouts and HipChat to help us build it all.

We are looking for people who share our values. We like to move quickly and ship lots of code, but in a considered way. We use TDD, CI and an all-hands-on-deck approach to Pull Requests to ensure we can keep the pace up without stinting on quality.

If any of us have a good idea for introducing a new technology or pattern then we go ahead and run an experiment. The debates don’t start until we have some real evidence to look at and since we avoid dogma and evangelism they tend to be short.

We work closely with our internal partners (no product managers here!) and we assume that they know best about what they need to do their jobs. Our job is to solve business problems and some of our proudest moments come from solving those problems without a line of code.
